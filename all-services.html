        Questions across every service

        Ask how the provider's written proposal addresses the complete requested scope.

        These questions apply whether the request concerns a single fixture or a complete bathroom remodel.

        Homeowners should independently verify licensing, insurance, references, applicable requirements, and other provider information before entering into an agreement.

        Ask for a written list of labor, preparation, materials, disposal, specialist work, fixtures, finish repair, protection, inspections, and exclusions.

        Clarify homeowner-supplied and provider-supplied items, allowances, ordering, delivery, inspection, storage, compatibility, damage, returns, and warranties.

        Discuss visible and concealed conditions, substrate damage, moisture, outdated systems, structural concerns, access limitations, and the procedure for documenting additional work.

        Ask about anticipated start windows, sequencing, working hours, dependencies, access, delays, updates, inspections, material lead times, and room usability.

        Ask which workmanship or product warranties the provider offers, their duration, coverage, exclusions, administration, and claim procedure.

        Clarify how changed conditions, revised selections, added work, pricing adjustments, schedule impacts, and homeowner approval will be recorded.
