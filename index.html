        Compare independently

        Ask for written details that make the proposed scope easier to compare.

        BathNice does not independently guarantee provider credentials or project outcomes. Homeowners should verify information before hiring.

        Ask for a written description of included labor, preparation, materials, exclusions, disposal, specialist work, and completion responsibilities.

        Review allowances, taxes, payment stages, contingencies, exclusions, validity periods, and how additional work would be priced.

        Discuss anticipated start windows, sequencing, access, working hours, dependencies, delays, communication, and practical use of the home.

        Verify applicable license and insurance requirements directly with the provider and relevant authorities before work begins.

        Ask which workmanship or product warranties are offered directly by the provider, what they cover, how long they apply, and how claims are handled.

        Clarify how changed conditions, revised selections, additional work, cost adjustments, and schedule changes will be documented and approved.

        Homeowners should independently verify licensing and insurance requirements.

        Prepare useful details

        A focused request can support a more useful first conversation.

        You do not need a finished design before reaching out. A clear description of the room and your priorities can give participating providers a practical starting point.

        • What you want to change

          Identify the room areas, fixtures, surfaces, or access concerns that matter most.

        • Preferred materials

          Note broad preferences while remaining open to provider-specific compatibility and availability.

        • Accessibility needs

          Describe current or future use priorities that should be discussed with qualified providers.

        • Timing considerations

          Share preferred windows, access restrictions, and any known dependencies without assuming a guaranteed date.

        • Questions for providers

          Prepare questions about scope, estimates, permits, scheduling, warranties, and change orders.
