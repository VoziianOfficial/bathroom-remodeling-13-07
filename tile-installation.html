        Tile project overview

        Sound preparation, water management, and deliberate layout support lasting tile.

        The project may involve shower walls, bathroom floors, feature walls, tub surrounds, repairs, or coordinated tile across several connected surfaces.

        Participating providers may evaluate substrates, movement, flatness, wet-area assemblies, drainage, penetrations, transitions, tile dimensions, layout priorities, and applicable requirements.

        Selecting tile is only one step. Substrate condition, waterproofing-related scope, layout, movement joints, trim, grout, and transitions should be confirmed before ordering.

        Final feasibility, preparation method, material suitability, pricing, delivery, specialist responsibilities, and agreement terms must be confirmed directly with the provider.

        Potential tile scope

        The written proposal should connect surface preparation, tile layout, setting materials, grout, trim, and transitions.

        Exact scope depends on existing surfaces, wet-area conditions, tile format, pattern, edge details, transitions, and provider evaluation.

        Surface and layout coordination
        01

        Removal and surface preparation

        The proposal may address removing existing finishes, protecting fixtures, checking flatness and movement, repairing surfaces, hauling debris, and documenting concealed conditions.

        02

        Tile format and layout

        Tile dimensions, pattern, grout width, focal lines, niches, penetrations, corners, waste cuts, and visual alignment may affect the layout.

        03

        Wet-area and drainage details

        Shower slopes, drain details, waterproofing-related scope, penetrations, curbs, benches, niches, and testing responsibilities may require coordination.

        04

        Substrate preparation

        Floor structure, wall backing, flatness, leveling, crack isolation, repair, priming, and tile-specific substrate requirements may be included.

        05

        Edges and adjacent finishes

        Profiles, bullnose, miters, caulk joints, base details, thresholds, paint edges, cabinetry, and transitions to adjacent flooring may affect scope.

        06

        Ordering, installation, and closeout

        Material quantities, dye lots, overage, delivery, inspection, storage, setting time, sealing, protection, care guidance, warranties, and closeout may be documented.

        This list is educational and does not represent a guaranteed or standardized tile-installation scope. Each provider supplies its own written proposal.

        Tile proposal checklist

        Connect preparation and layout to the complete written installation scope.

        A detailed proposal can clarify material responsibility, preparation, layout, setting materials, grout, trim, transitions, protection, and closeout.

        Homeowners should independently verify licensing, insurance, references, applicable requirements, and other provider information before entering into an agreement.

        Confirm manufacturer, series, tile dimensions, finish, shade variation, slip characteristics where relevant, setting materials, grout, trim, sealers, and approved substitutions.

        Ask who verifies surface dimensions, tile quantities, overage, dye lots, substrate compatibility, wet-area use, transitions, delivery, storage, and installation access.

        Review removal, disposal, fixture protection, substrate repair, flatness, leveling, crack isolation, backing, priming, and concealed-condition procedures.

        Clarify slopes, drains, penetrations, waterproofing-related work, movement joints, testing, specialist work, manufacturer requirements, and inspection responsibilities.

        Ask which patterns, cuts, niches, corners, profiles, grout, movement and caulk joints, sealing, thresholds, base details, and transitions are included or excluded.

        Review who orders and inspects tile, setting materials and trim, delivery and storage, returns, lead times, quantities, allowances, payment stages, scheduling, changes, product warranties, and workmanship warranties.
