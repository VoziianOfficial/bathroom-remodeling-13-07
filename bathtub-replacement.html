        Bathtub project overview

        A replacement tub must fit the room, the plumbing, and the way the bathroom will be used.

        The project may involve replacing a similar alcove tub, changing to a freestanding design, exploring an easier-access option, or restoring surrounding finishes.

        Participating providers may evaluate the existing tub, room dimensions, plumbing locations, floor structure, wall conditions, delivery route, fixture compatibility, drainage, access priorities, and applicable requirements.

        Selecting the bathtub is only one step. Dimensions, support, plumbing, access, and surrounding restoration should be confirmed before ordering.

        Final feasibility, installation method, product compatibility, pricing, delivery, permits, specialist responsibilities, and agreement terms must be confirmed directly with the provider.

        Potential bathtub scope

        The written proposal should cover both the new tub and the changes required to install it.

        Exact scope depends on the existing fixture, new tub type, room construction, plumbing, access, and provider evaluation.

        Tub and room compatibility
        01

        Removal and disposal

        The proposal may address disconnecting fixtures, removing the existing tub, protecting nearby surfaces, managing access, hauling materials, and documenting concealed conditions.

        02

        Dimensions and clearances

        Tub length, width, height, bathing depth, wall spacing, fixture clearance, door movement, circulation, and maintenance access may affect the selection.

        03

        Plumbing and drainage

        Drain location, overflow, water supply, faucet type, valve access, connection changes, testing, and specialist responsibilities may require coordination.

        04

        Support and preparation

        Floor structure, leveling, bedding, framing, wall repair, substrate preparation, access panels, and product-specific support requirements may be included.

        05

        Surrounds and adjacent finishes

        Tile, wall panels, trim, waterproofing-related work, floor repair, paint, base details, transitions, and restoration around the new tub may affect scope.

        06

        Ordering, delivery, and closeout

        Product responsibility, delivery route, inspection, storage, damage, returns, installation timing, warranty information, cleaning, and closeout may be documented.

        This list is educational and does not represent a guaranteed or standardized bathtub-replacement scope. Each provider supplies its own written proposal.

        Bathtub proposal checklist

        Ask how the selected tub connects to every part of the written replacement scope.

        A detailed proposal can clarify product responsibility, compatibility, preparation, delivery, installation, and finish restoration.

        Homeowners should independently verify licensing, insurance, references, applicable requirements, and other provider information before entering into an agreement.

        Confirm manufacturer, model, dimensions, material, finish, drain orientation, overflow, faucet type, accessories, product requirements, and approved substitutions.

        Ask who verifies room dimensions, rough opening, finished clearances, drain position, supply locations, fixture compatibility, support, delivery path, and installation access.

        Review disconnection, removal, disposal, floor and wall protection, substrate repair, leveling, framing, bedding, reinforcement, access panels, and concealed-condition procedures.

        Clarify drain, overflow, supply, faucet, valve, testing, connection changes, specialist work, manufacturer requirements, access, and inspection responsibilities.

        Ask which wall, tile, panel, trim, floor, base, waterproofing-related, painting, caulking, sealing, transition, and restoration work is included or excluded.

        Review who orders and inspects the tub, delivery and storage, returns, lead times, total pricing, allowances, payment stages, scheduling, changes, product warranties, and workmanship warranties.
