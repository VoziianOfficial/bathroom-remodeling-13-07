        About BathNice

        An independent platform for exploring bathroom-remodeling provider options.

        01

        Clarity can come before a bathroom project contract.

        BathNice gives homeowners a structured place to describe a bathroom-remodeling need and explore possible conversations with participating providers.

        The platform supports the introduction. Providers remain independent, and homeowners retain responsibility for reviewing information and making the final hiring decision.

        Why the platform exists

        Early remodeling decisions often need structure, not pressure.

        Bathroom projects can involve several connected conversations: layout, surfaces, fixtures, storage, accessibility, electrical work, ventilation, timing, and budget expectations.

        BathNice was designed to make that first step more useful. Instead of presenting products for sale or pretending to be the company performing the work, the platform helps homeowners organize project information for potential provider conversations.

        It does not require a finished design. A clear description of the room, the problems to solve, and the homeowner's priorities can be enough to begin.

        Clear boundaries

        What BathNice can support—and what remains outside its role.

        BathNice can help with

        A more organized first step

        • Collecting project details

          The request can describe the room, service type, priorities, timing considerations, and contact information.

        • Supporting possible introductions

          Submitted information may be shared with participating providers based on location and availability.

        • Preparing comparison questions

          BathNice provides educational guidance about scope, estimates, schedules, credentials, and change orders.

        • Clarifying the next step

          Homeowners can use the platform to begin a conversation without creating an automatic service agreement.

        BathNice does not

        Replace independent verification

        • Perform remodeling work

          BathNice does not install fixtures, tile surfaces, vanities, showers, tubs, or accessibility features.

        • Employ participating providers

          Providers are independent businesses and are not BathNice employees, agents, or remodeling crews.

        • Establish final project pricing

          Estimates, payment terms, allowances, and change costs are supplied directly by providers.

        • Guarantee credentials or outcomes

          Homeowners remain responsible for verifying licensing, insurance, references, applicable requirements, and final contract terms.

        A measured beginning

        BathNice does not make the decision. It helps prepare the first conversation.

        Provider availability, project feasibility, final pricing, schedules, warranties, and service terms remain provider-specific.

        Information to request

        Clear provider information can make comparisons more practical.

        The topics below are educational prompts. BathNice does not verify every statement supplied by a participating provider.

        Homeowners should independently verify licensing, insurance, references, applicable requirements, and other provider information before entering into an agreement.

        Ask which demolition, preparation, installation, finishing, disposal, specialist work, materials, inspections, and closeout tasks are included or excluded.

        Review labor, materials, allowances, taxes, deposit requirements, payment stages, estimate validity, exclusions, and the treatment of unforeseen work.

        Discuss anticipated start windows, working hours, sequencing, access, dependencies, delays, update frequency, and the main communication contact.

        Ask for applicable license and insurance information, then independently confirm current requirements and validity with relevant authorities or insurers.

        Clarify whether workmanship or product warranties are offered, what they cover, who administers them, how long they apply, and which exclusions may apply.

        Ask how revised scope, hidden conditions, additional work, cost changes, schedule changes, and approval responsibilities will be documented.

        Responsibility remains distinct

        The platform, provider, and homeowner each have a different role.

        Keeping those roles separate supports more transparent project conversations.

        BathNice responsibilities

        Platform information
        Explain the independent provider-matching role in clear language.
        Request collection
        Receive the project and contact information submitted by the homeowner.
        Possible matching
        Share relevant information with participating providers when appropriate and available.
        Educational guidance
        Offer useful planning and provider-comparison topics without guaranteeing the provider.
        02

        Provider responsibilities

        Project evaluation
        Assess the room, requested scope, feasibility, applicable requirements, and specialist needs.
        Provider information
        Supply accurate business, credential, insurance, reference, and service information.
        Proposal and terms
        Present scope, pricing, scheduling, payment, warranties, exclusions, and change procedures.
        Contract performance
        Fulfill obligations established directly with the homeowner under the final agreement.
        03

        Homeowner responsibilities

        Accurate request details
        Share useful information about the property, room, priorities, access, and timing.
        Independent verification
        Review applicable licensing, insurance, references, requirements, and provider representations.
        Proposal comparison
        Examine written scope, pricing, schedules, warranties, exclusions, and contract language.
        Final decision
        Choose whether to enter into an agreement directly with a selected provider.
        About the platform

        Four important questions about the BathNice model.

        No. BathNice is an independent provider-matching platform. It does not directly remodel bathrooms, install fixtures, employ participating providers, or enter into remodeling agreements with homeowners.

        No. Participating providers operate independently. BathNice does not supervise their work, control their schedules, establish their prices, or guarantee their credentials or project outcomes.

        Project and contact information submitted through the BathNice request form may be shared with participating providers who may be available to discuss the requested bathroom project.

        The homeowner independently decides whether to continue with a participating provider. Any final agreement is made directly between the homeowner and the selected provider.

        Submitting a request does not create a service agreement and does not guarantee that a provider will contact you.
