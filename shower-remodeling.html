        Shower project overview

        A shower remodel connects visible details with drainage, preparation, and wet-area responsibilities.

        The project may focus on easier entry, a different enclosure, updated surfaces, additional storage, improved controls, or a more complete shower-area rebuild.

        Participating providers may evaluate room dimensions, existing plumbing, drain position, floor conditions, structure, wall preparation, ventilation, electrical needs, glass dimensions, support features, and applicable requirements.

        A useful request describes how the shower should feel easier to enter, use, clean, and maintain.

        Final dimensions, drainage, waterproofing-related work, structure, glass requirements, support, pricing, and feasibility must be evaluated directly by qualified providers.

        Potential shower scope

        A written proposal should connect the visible shower with the work required beneath and around it.

        Exact scope depends on the existing shower, desired entry, room construction, material choices, and provider evaluation.

        06

        Enclosure type, glass measurements, door swing, hardware finish, support, lead time, adjacent wall or floor repair, cleaning, and closeout may be addressed.

        Shower proposal checklist

        Connect every shower decision to the written installation scope.

        A detailed proposal can make it easier to understand responsibilities for preparation, drainage, fixtures, glass, surfaces, and finishing.

        Homeowners should independently verify licensing, insurance, references, applicable requirements, and other provider information before entering into an agreement.

        Ask what existing materials will be removed, which nearby surfaces will be protected, who handles disposal, and how concealed conditions or damage will be documented.

        Clarify the proposed drain, base or floor approach, slope, substrate preparation, wet-area system, transitions, testing, documentation, and which party is responsible for each part.

        Review surface materials, pattern, layout approval, cuts, edges, trim, grout, movement joints, sealing, maintenance, and the treatment of adjacent surfaces.

        Confirm fixture models, valve compatibility, control placement, showerhead height, niches, shelving, benches, wall reinforcement, support features, and supply responsibilities.

        Ask who measures, orders, supplies, installs, and warranties the glass; confirm door movement, hardware, support, water containment, lead time, and exclusions.

        Review total pricing, allowances, deposits, payment stages, material lead times, schedule assumptions, change-order procedures, workmanship warranties, and product warranty responsibilities.
